# Literature Note: Khuntia et al. (2019)

**Title**: Radial flow and differential freeze-out in proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 7$ TeV at the LHC
**DOI**: 10.1140/epja/i2019-12669-6
**arXiv**: 1808.02383
**Key Findings**:
1. Analyzes $p_T$ spectra as a function of charged-particle multiplicity at mid-rapidity ($|y| < 0.5$) for identified particles ($\pi, K, p, \phi, \Lambda$, etc.).
2. Uses Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave (BGBW) and thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution.
3. **$T_{\text{kin}}$ (Kinetic Freeze-out Temperature)**: Exhibits mild dependence on multiplicity class.
4. **$\beta$ (Radial Flow)**: Shows almost independent behavior across multiplicity classes.
5. **Tsallis $q$-parameter**: Approaches the Boltzmann-Gibbs value (thermal equilibrium) as multiplicity increases.
6. **Mass Ordering**: Observed in temperature and non-extensivity parameter, supporting a differential freeze-out scenario.

**Relevance to AiSci**:
- Provides a baseline for BGBW and Tsallis fits in pp collisions at 7 TeV.
- The multiplicity independence of $\beta$ is a key benchmark for Robert's 3-component model.
