# Literature Note: Rath et al. (2020)

**Title**: Event multiplicity, transverse momentum and energy dependence of charged particle production, and system thermodynamics in $pp$ collisions at the Large Hadron Collider
**DOI**: 10.1088/1361-6471/ab783b
**arXiv**: 1908.04208
**Key Findings**:
1. Studies collision energy (5.02 and 13 TeV) and multiplicity dependence of charged particle $p_T$ spectra.
2. Compares Tsallis and Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave (BGBW) models.
3. **$\langle \beta \rangle$ (Average Radial Flow)**: Almost independent of collision energy and multiplicity.
4. **$T_{\text{kin}}$**: Significantly depends on multiplicity classes.
5. **Model Comparison**: Tsallis describes the full $p_T$ range better, while BGBW is effective for the bulk part ($p_T \lesssim 2.5$ GeV/c).
6. Verification of BGBW applicability in different multiplicity classes at LHC energies.

**Relevance to AiSci**:
- Validates the use of BGBW for the "bulk" component of the spectra.
- Provides a reference for $T_{\text{kin}}$ vs multiplicity at the highest LHC energies (13 TeV), which can be compared to Robert's fit parameters.
