## Summary

The paper proposes a moving-system boson probability model and applies it to ATLAS 13 TeV data. Current local checks suggest that parts of the kinematic structure are internally consistent under Boltzmann-like, massless or ultra-relativistic assumptions, but this is not yet a full validation of the manuscript. The statistical support, fit stability, and exact relation to a full Bose-Einstein treatment still need stronger evidence.

## Major Concerns

1. The manuscript should report chi2/ndf for every fitted multiplicity bin.
2. The high-multiplicity bins appear poorly constrained based on very large parameter uncertainties; an independent refit is needed before assigning a root cause.
3. The paper should state clearly whether the fitted formula is full Bose-Einstein or a Boltzmann/Juttner approximation.
4. The paper should compare the model with standard phenomenological baselines such as literature-matched Tsallis/Tsallis-Pareto and Blast-Wave fits.
5. The discussion should explain whether any fitted velocity trend is physically meaningful or an artifact of fit range, parameter bounds, and parameter degeneracy.
6. The paper should include complete uncertainty tables and parameter correlation information.

## Minor Concerns

- Equation numbering and cross-references should be checked after the final manuscript export.
- Figure captions should state fit range, excluded data regions, approximations, and parameter constraints.
