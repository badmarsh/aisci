\begin{abstract}
This thesis presents an autonomous, multi-agent AI framework designed to rigorously validate phenomenological physics models and literature claims. The framework is applied to evaluate a proposed 3-component model for transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra of bosons produced in high-energy collisions. We definitively prove the intrinsic over-parameterization of the 2-component J\"uttner model for high-multiplicity $p_T$ spectra, contrasting its failure to converge against robust Blast-Wave (BGBW) and Tsallis baselines. Our framework seamlessly integrates Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and Model Context Protocol (MCP) toolchains, bridging the gap between symbolic validation, numerical fitting, and literature consensus.
\end{abstract}
